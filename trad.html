What Is a Traditional IRA?


Traditional IRA meaning

A Traditional IRA(Indivdual Retirement Account) is an account that offers individuals to contribute PRE-tax dollars towards their retirement savings. Since this is pre-tax, any earnings made in a Traditional IRA are subject to taxes. So, what are the benefits of a traditional ira?

Benefits of a Traditional IRA

The major benefit of a Traditional IRA is that all contributions to your IRA are tax-deductable for that contributed year. This is especially useful for those who are self-employed and want to help mitigate their tax-threshold and also have a retirement account.

It also offers a wide range of investment options such as stocks, bonds, mutual funds and more. This allows you to customize your investment strategy based on your own risk tolerance and goals.

Tax deferred growth allows the money invested into the account be tax-deferred as it grows and you will only pay taxes on the earnings at withdrawal.

Withdrawing Before 59.

Although it is not recommended to withdraw money, emergencies can happen. If you make any withdrawal before 59, you may have to pay taxes and penalties on those earnings.

Only withdraw the amount you need, if you withdraw too much, you may not be able to place it back into the account since contributions are capped at $6,000.00 a year.

When/How to open up a Traditional IRA

The sooner the better however, dependent on your earnings, it is never to late to help mitigate your taxes due at the end of the year by looking at opening a Traditional IRA, it can help you save taxes and use that money for yourself!
